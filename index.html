Non-myopic robot exploration through spatial-temporal reasoning

Abstract

Autonomous exploration of unknown environments remains challenging for mobile robots, primarily due to myopic decision-making and limited long-term planning capability. Existing methods frequently decouple spatial perception from historical information, leading to redundant revisits and degraded exploration efficiency.

To address these challenges, we propose SEKIRO, a Structure-Enhanced and Koopman-Integrated Deep Reinforcement Learning framework that unifies spatial and temporal reasoning for sustained exploration. Specifically, a GNN-based spatial embedding block captures multi-scale region dependencies through skeleton-driven region division and a structural encroaching strategy. A Koopman operator-based temporal embedding block encodes the evolution of exploration history into a linear latent space via a quasi-diagonal state matrix, enabling efficient long-horizon reasoning. An attention-integrated policy network fuses these representations for non-myopic decision-making, with a dual-stage planning scheme generating precise local exploration paths and global guidance paths.

Extensive simulations are carried out in three different environments. The results demonstrate that SEKIRO achieves superior exploration efficiency, coverage completeness, and runtime balance over state-of-the-art benchmarks. Real-world experiments further validate the practical feasibility of the proposed framework.

Key Contributions

  1. A GNN-based spatial embedding network is developed to capture spatial and multi-scale dependencies across exploring regions, in which a skeleton-driven region division mechanism is proposed to generate topology-consistent regions and beacon points, while a structural encroaching strategy is proposed to quantify regional structural features.
  2. A Koopman operator-based temporal embedding network is designed to extract temporal features from historical exploration information, where the temporal evolution of node features on the incremental sparse topological graph is encoded into a linear latent space, enabling efficient long-horizon temporal reasoning and improving the consistency of sequential exploration decisions.
  3. An attention integrated DRL policy is constructed to jointly exploit graph spatial and temporal features for non-myopic decision-making, over which a dual-stage planning scheme is performed to restore regional structural details from the target point and generate high-precision local exploration paths, while maintaining global connectivity and exploration continuity through fast graph-based search among unexplored regions.

Method Overview

Fig. 1. Overview of the proposed SEKIRO framework with three individual blocks: Node Spatial Embedding Block, Node Temporal Embedding Block, and Policy and Planning Block.

SEKIRO is a structure-enhanced and Koopman-integrated deep reinforcement learning framework that unifies spatial and temporal reasoning for non-myopic robot exploration. It captures multi-scale region dependencies through a GNN-based spatial embedding block and encodes exploration history into a linear latent space via a Koopman operator, enabling efficient long-horizon planning.

SEKIRO consists of three main components:

(1) Spatial Embedding Block: A skeleton-driven region division constructs a sparse global graph Gg, and a structural encroaching strategy quantifies regional features into a comprehensive descriptor. A cascaded GNN (GCN + GATv2) captures multi-scale spatial dependencies across regions.

(2) Temporal Embedding Block: A Knowledge Pool stores historical information, and a Multi-EMA mechanism refines exploration knowledge. A quasi-diagonal formed Koopman operator encodes temporal evolution into a linear latent space, enabling efficient long-horizon reasoning.

(3) Policy and Planning Block: An attention mechanism fuses the robot's current state with both spatial and temporal embeddings for navigation point determination. A dual-stage planning scheme generates a high-precision local exploration path along encroaching layers and a global guidance path using ATSP on the global graph.

Real-World Experiments

Fig. 6. Real-world experiment scenes and exploration results. (a, c) Schematics of Loop and Hall environments reconstructed using a handheld scanner, with representative real-world views and non-traversable areas (e.g., stairs and slopes) annotated. To validate practical applicability, we conduct field experiments using a Scout Mini wheeled robot equipped with an onboard PC (i5-1340P CPU and 16 GB of RAM), an MTI-30 IMU, and an RS-LiDAR-32. The terrain map in [15] is adopted for traversability analysis, and the trained model is deployed in a zero-shot manner without additional tuning or retraining. All computations were run onboard, with max velocity limited to 0.6m/s for safety.

Experimental Environments:

  • Loop: Loop-shaped corridor with intersections — completed in 373.10s, 163.38m movement, covering 2153.38m²
  • Hall: Building entrance hall with spatial scale variations — completed in 172.04s, 79.64m movement, covering 5284.63m²

Results demonstrate that SEKIRO generates effective exploration strategies at intersections and handles rapid changes in spatial scale, validating the practical feasibility and zero-shot capability of the proposed framework.

Conclusion

In this work, we proposed SEKIRO, a structure-enhanced and Koopman-integrated deep reinforcement learning framework for robot exploration of unknown environments. Rather than treating spatial perception and temporal reasoning in isolation, SEKIRO encodes multi-scale region dependencies through a skeleton-driven region division and a structural encroaching strategy, while simultaneously capturing the evolution of exploration history in a linear latent space derived from the Koopman operator. An attention mechanism fuses these spatial and temporal representations to support non-myopic decision-making, and a dual-stage planning scheme further generates high-precision local exploration paths while maintaining global connectivity and exploration continuity across unexplored regions. Extensive simulations across three representative scenarios demonstrate that SEKIRO achieves superior coverage, efficiency, and responsiveness compared to state-of-the-art benchmarks. Real-world experiments further validate its practicality and potential for application in industrial intelligent systems.

Nevertheless, the current framework is designed for a single robot operating in static environments, which may restrict its applicability to more complex real-world scenarios. Future work will extend SEKIRO to a multi-robot system and integrate vision-language models (VLMs) to enable semantic-aware decision-making that adapts to dynamic and semantically rich environments.
